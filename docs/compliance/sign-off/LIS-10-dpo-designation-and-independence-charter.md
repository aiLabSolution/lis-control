## 1. Designation

LabSolution hereby designates **Kirsten Pinote** as its **Data Protection Officer (DPO)**, effective
on the date of signature below. The DPO's name and contact details are to be registered with the
National Privacy Commission via the **NPCRS** as part of LabSolution's registration / sworn-declaration
obligations (NPC Circular 2022-04).

## 2. Scope of the role

The DPO is accountable for data-protection compliance across LabSolution's processing of personal
data, specifically:

- **All deployment models** — LabSolution's **own corporate processing** (staff, customer, and
  business-contact data), for which LabSolution is a **Personal Information Controller (PIC)**.
- **The M3 post-pilot spoke** — the PHI that LabSolution will process as a **Personal Information
  Processor (PIP)** when the central-sync spoke goes live (per ADR-0006 / the M3 compliance gate).
- **The M1 pilot** — although LabSolution is *neither PIC nor PIP for the lab's PHI* at M1, the DPO
  oversees the **scoped support / break-glass DPA** and confirms the load-bearing "no PHI access"
  premise holds (DEC-17).

The **customer laboratory/hospital remains the PIC** for the patient data in the LIS and designates
**its own** DPO; this charter does not cover the customer's DPO obligation.

## 3. Independence & reporting line  *(IRR Sec. 26 — load-bearing)*

> **Independence note (required).** The designated DPO, **Kirsten Pinote**, shares a surname with the
> accountable QA/regulatory owner, **Artis Lindy Pinote**. To preempt any NPC independence challenge,
> this charter **expressly documents** the DPO's independence:

- The DPO **does not decide the purposes or means** of LabSolution's processing and holds **no role**
  that conflicts with data-protection duties (e.g. is not the system/technical owner who controls the
  processing systems).
- The DPO has a **direct reporting line to the highest level of management** (the accountable owner /
  the board) and reports **functionally independent** of the engineering and commercial functions.
- The DPO **cannot be penalised** for performing DPO duties and is given the autonomy to raise issues
  directly to management and to the NPC.
- Where a potential conflict arises on a specific matter, it is escalated and documented, and external
  counsel is engaged.

| Reports to | Independent of | Conflict-of-interest control |
|---|---|---|
| Accountable owner / board | Engineering (system owner) + commercial functions | No purpose-setting role; documented escalation path; counsel on conflict |

## 4. Duties (RA 10173 IRR Sec. 26 — confirm exact enumeration with counsel)

- Monitor LabSolution's compliance with RA 10173, its IRR, and NPC issuances.
- Advise on Privacy Impact Assessments (PIA) and Records of Processing Activities (RoPA) for
  LabSolution's own / PIP processing.
- Serve as **contact point** for the NPC and for data subjects.
- Oversee **personal-data-breach** management (assess, contain, and support notification within the
  NPC-prescribed window — DEC-12) and LabSolution's own Security Incident Management apparatus (M3).
- Cultivate awareness and oversee data-protection training.
- Coordinate with the accountable QA/regulatory owner on the security-posture sign-off and the
  Stage-5 pen-test scope (REQ-SEC-04).

## 5. Resources & access

LabSolution shall provide the DPO with the access, information, training, and budget reasonably
necessary to perform the role, including access to processing activities, records, and the engineering
and security teams.

## 6. Term & review

Effective on signature; reviewed at least annually and upon any material change to LabSolution's
processing, topology, or the M1→M3 transition. Supersedes any prior designation.
